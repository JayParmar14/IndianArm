CHUSHUL WAR MEMORIAL DHRUVA DARJEELING WAR MEMORIAL KARGIL WAR MEMORIAL

Chushul War Memorial, made to commemorate the sacrifice and bravery of Gurkh Tps during the battle of Sirijap in the 1962 Indo-China War.

Dhruva, a monument at Udhampur is dedicated to the brave soldiers of Northern Command. It was inaugurated on 17 June 1997 by the then COAS Gen S Roychowdhury, PVSM, ADC on the occasion of the Silver Jubilee of the Command.

The War Memorial has been created to honor and to serve as a memorial for the Gorkha soldiers of Darjeeling area who sacrificed their lives in various operations and wars after India's independence.

The Kargil war Memorial was constructed at “Ground Zero” location at Dras in year 2004 as an act of apt recognition to the supreme sacrifice made by our brave soldiers during ‘Operation Vijay’.
MARWAR MEMORIAL REZANG LA WAR MEMORIAL SOUTH WESTERN COMMAND MEMORIAL WALONG WAR MEMORIAL

The Marwar Memorial has been constructed in memory of soldiers who sacrificed their lives in action on 18 December 1961 during ‘Op Vijay’, for the liberation of Diu Island and compelled the Portugese Governor Major Fernando D’ Almeda E-Vasconcels to surrender the Island of Diu to the Union of India.

Rezang La War Memorial, located a few kilometers after the town of Chushul, was created in the remembrance of the brave soldiers of 13 Battalion of Kumaon Regiment, deployed under the leadership of Major Shaitan Singh, during the 1962 Indo-China War.

Dograi war memorial located at Khasa, Amritsar is dedicated to brave soldiers who laid their lives for winning one of the toughest battles fought during 1965 Indo-Pak War.

Walong War Memorial commemorates the fierce Battle of Walong in 1962 when the outnumbered Indian Army’s 11th Infantry Brigade blocked the thrust of the invading Chinese.
